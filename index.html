Introduction

Hello guys! My name is Cecilia and I am a graduating freshman studying Operations Research Management Science at UC Berkeley. I entered Cal as an international student from Toronto, Canada. One of the similar things I noticed upon arrival was that Cal also has a variety of boba places that are comparable to the places at home. During my free time as a freshman, I like to explore different boba places around our campus. I have compiled my own rating list of the Boba restaurants near Berkeley. Check them out in the Boba Ratings section! For detailed reviews of certain places, see Reviews!

ShareTea

Description: The typical place for social gatherings due to location proximity to campus, Sharetea has the classic flavors and the wholesome set of boba choices you can find.

Perks: Variety of options, close location to Sproul, efficient service

Downside: Very platonic, no "innovative" flavors and drinks, use dairy cremer in most of their drinks

Ucha

Description: Close to RSF and Downtown Berkeley, this is a perfect place to go if you want to pick up a drink after workout or on your way towards Downtown Berkeley. UCha is known for its high quality drinks and ingredients.

Perks: Super tasty, uses fresh milk upon request, boba is very fresh and chewy, high-quality customer service

Downside: Pricey, limited seating inside

Plentea

Description: Plentea is super close to the residential units and is known for its signature drinks in the glass bottle packaging. If you ever want the experience of drinking boba in a manner of living the "boba lifestyle", grab a glass bottle and take Instagram photos! If you bring back the bottle the next time you order, you will receive 10% off.

Perks: Close to campus, heavily-experience oriented, high-quality tea imported from Taiwan, more dairy options compared to others, option to order a "re-make" if you are not happy with your drink

Downside: Slow service, no seatings inside, tea is a little bit bitter, pricey, glass bottle cracks in contact with hot water

Sweetheart Cafe

Description: Located in Asian Ghetto, this boba place is a hidden gem. Not only does it serve classic milk tea, but also other deserts like mango sago and popcorn chicken that compliments boba. The owners are really nice and you can easily get a discount by chatting with them in a friendly manner.

Perks: Cheap price, variety of options, cash discounts

Downside: Sanitation issues, slow serivce

TeaOne Telegraph

Description: If you live in the southside of campus, stop by TeaOne on Telegraph as you walk home! TeaOne differentiates from other boba places with their fruit drinks. You can easily customize the sugar and ice levels and tailor your drink to what you exactly want!

Perks: Very innovative boba drinks, new release of flavors every two months, fast service

Downside: Smell of weed inside

TeaOne Cory

Description: Similar to TeaOne on Telegraph, this location also serve as a boba hub for students to enjoy in the engineering building. Taking a study break? Come to Cory to grab a drink and study!

Perks: Nice seating areas in the middle of Cory courtyard, very good place to study, location advantage for students on the north campus.

Downside: Some flavors not offered here, less variety and longer wait than the store on Telegraph

UCafe

Description: UCafe is the closest boba place to Unit 1 and Unit 2. It has the basic flavors if you are really cravy something sweet and lazy to walk a decent amount of distance to Asian Ghetto.

Perks: Cheap price, can customize drink easily, well sanitation maintenence

Downside: Low quality tapioca, fresh milk subsituted by milk and dairy powder, very sugar-heavy, limited seating inside

Sheng Kee Bakery

Description: Running late to your morning 8am? Don't forget to still eat breakfast! While you come grab a piece of freshly made pastry, you can always order a freshly brewed drink to start off your day! Sheng Kee boba are made with Hong Kong style for those of you who enjoy it. It has a heavy taste of milk and slight bitterness that really spicens up the overall experience.

Perks: The speed at which they make the drinks is faster here than anywhere else, really good price for the quality of boba, free add-on toppings

Downside: Less customization offered

85 C

Description: 85 C is another bakery similar to Sheng Kee but located in downtown. The milk tea here are comparable quality to Share Tea with less options as it is a bakery store.

Perks: Resonable price, numerous seatings inside to chat and study

Downside: The waiting time here is the worst out of all the reviewed posts on this page. The baristas are poorly trained to make drinks efficiently under a resonable amount of time period. Furthermore, they often mess up customers' orders and make the wrong drink or customization after taking up a substantial amount of time. However, if you are patient enough, they are more than happy to re-make the drink for you--correctly, this time.

Asha Tea

Description: Asha Tea is one of the higher-end boba places in Berkeley. Similar to Plentea, the tea ingredients are imported from Taiwan and brewed meticulously to reach the desired flavor. This place is on the next block of 85 C in downtown, so if you ever want a change in experience, come drop by with a friend and enjoy the atmosphere this store has to offer.

Perks: Very comfortable environment to study or have a serious chat, variety of flavors, highest quality drink on this rating page

Downside: Tapioca not as ideal as the drink, pricey for the quantity of drink they serve you

Peak Point

Description: Peakpoint is a popular hot-pot restaurant near Unit 2. However, their boba drink is very well-served that it deserves to be featured among other boba specialty places. Although there are limited flavors of boab, any drink will complement well with the spice of hotpots.

Perks: Huge quantity served, really good customer service, drink can be easily customized, toppings are reputable amongst other boba specialty places

Downside: Sugar levels may vary a lot depending on the barista

Tasty Pot

Description: Another popular hot-pot restaurant in downtown, Tasty Pot gets really busy on the weekends during dining hours. Make sure to come here before 6pm if you don't want to wait half an hour for seatings! If you do plan on having boba as a side drink, make sure to not order from other places and bring it in! Sadly, they will not allow you to have that inside the restaurant and will put it away for you until you pay the check.

Perks: Decent price, good complement with their signature hotpots, refreshing compared to the creamy taste of Share Tea and Ucha

Downside: Slow service, cannot customize drink, bland flavors
